# Great Ocean Road

## Beaches and swimming

Lorne's main beach is patrolled in summer and stays relatively calm, making it
the family pick along this stretch of coast. Apollo Bay has a long, open beach
better suited to walking than swimming when the swell is up. For quieter water,
the small coves around Wye River and Kennett River are worth the short drive.
Always check the flags and swim between them — this is exposed ocean coastline.

## Walks and lookouts

The Great Ocean Walk runs in day-sized sections between Apollo Bay and the
Twelve Apostles; you can pick a single leg without committing to the whole
route. Closer to Lorne, the Erskine Falls track is a short rainforest walk to a
tall waterfall, busiest mid-morning. Teddy's Lookout gives a classic view of the
road curving along the coast and is a five-minute stroll from the car park.

## Wildlife

Kennett River is the reliable spot for wild koalas — look up into the manna gums
along Grey River Road early or late in the day. King parrots and crimson
rosellas are common here too. Kangaroos graze the Anglesea golf course at dusk.
Keep well back from all wildlife and never feed them; human food makes them sick.

## Wet-weather options

When the coast is socked in, the rainforest actually shows best in the rain: the
Maits Rest boardwalk near Apollo Bay is a short, sheltered loop through ancient
myrtle beech. The Otway Fly treetop walk stays open in drizzle. Lorne and Apollo
Bay both have small cinemas and cafes that make an easy afternoon when the
weather turns.

## Campgrounds and facilities

Big holiday parks in Lorne and Apollo Bay have powered sites, camp kitchens and
laundries, and book out fast over summer and long weekends — reserve well ahead.
More basic national-park campgrounds sit inland in the Otways with pit toilets
and no power. Dump points are available at the larger parks. Mobile coverage is
patchy once you leave the main towns.

## Local tips and FAQs

The road is winding and slow — plan for far less distance per hour than a
highway, especially towing. Fuel up in the larger towns; stations thin out
between them. Summer and Easter are peak: expect full car parks at the Twelve
Apostles by late morning, so go early or late for both light and space. Many
kitchens stop dinner service earlier than city hours out of season.
