# Margaret River

## Wineries and produce

The region is one of Australia's best-known wine areas, with cellar doors ranging
from big estates to small family labels, most open through the day for tastings
and long lunches. Between the vines are breweries, a distillery or two, and
producers of cheese, chocolate, olive oil and fudge that make an easy self-drive
food trail. Build in a designated driver or a tour, and check cellar-door hours —
many close mid to late afternoon.

## Beaches and surf

The coast here is wild and beautiful. Surfers Point at Prevelly hosts world-tour
surfing and throws powerful waves for the experienced; Gnarabup and Hamelin Bay
are calmer for swimming, and Hamelin Bay is known for the stingrays that glide
into the shallows. The water is cooler and the surf stronger than the east coast,
so swim where patrolled or sheltered and respect the conditions.

## Caves, forests and walks

Inland, a line of limestone caves — Lake, Jewel, Mammoth and Ngilgi — run tours
through underground chambers, a good all-weather option. The towering karri
forests around Boranup and the walk trails of the Cape to Cape track along the
coast between the Naturaliste and Leeuwin lighthouses give the region its best
walking, from short lookouts to multi-day sections.

## Wet-weather options

A wet day is easy here: the caves are underground and unaffected, the cellar doors,
breweries and produce stops are all indoors, and the galleries and cafes of
Margaret River town and Dunsborough fill the gaps. The karri forest drives are
atmospheric in mist and rain. Winter brings the region's rain and swell, so pack
layers and a rain shell and lean on the indoor food-and-wine trail.

## Campgrounds and facilities

Holiday parks sit around Margaret River town, Prevelly, Dunsborough and Augusta,
with powered sites, camp kitchens, dump points and laundries; national-park and
forest campgrounds near Boranup offer a quieter, basic stay among the karris. Book
ahead for summer and school holidays. Some coast and forest tracks are narrow or
unsealed, so check access suits your rig before turning off the main roads.

## Local tips and FAQs

The region is busiest and priciest over summer and WA school holidays; spring and
autumn are quieter and pleasant. Surf beaches carry strong waves and cold water —
swim at patrolled or sheltered spots. Always plan a sober driver for wine touring
and check cellar-door and cave hours, as many close mid-afternoon. Distances
between stops are larger than they look, so map the day and fuel up in town.
