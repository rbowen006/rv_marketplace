# Noosa

## Beaches and swimming

Noosa Main Beach is one of the few north-facing beaches on the east coast, which
makes it calm, patrolled and good for families and learner surfers. Around the
headland, a string of small coves — Tea Tree and Granite Bay — reward the walk
with clearer water and gentle point surf. Sunshine Beach on the ocean side is
wilder and better for confident swimmers. Swim between the flags on the open
stretches.

## National park and walks

Noosa National Park runs right off the end of Hastings Street, and the coastal
track around the headland is the region's signature walk — an easy couple of
hours past the coves to the wave-cut Boiling Pot and Hell's Gates, with koalas
often in the trees and turtles and dolphins offshore. Longer loops climb into the
tea-tree forest. Start early to beat the heat and the crowds.

## Rivers, everglades and food

Behind the beaches, the Noosa River and the Everglades of the Noosa hinterland
open up for kayaking, paddleboarding and calm-water cruises through rare mirrored
waterways. Hastings Street and the Noosa Junction have the region's dining, and
the Eumundi Markets inland run Wednesday and Saturday mornings for crafts and
produce. The hinterland villages of Montville and Maleny add cool-climate cafes
and lookouts.

## Wet-weather options

Rain suits the hinterland: the drive up to Montville and Maleny, the Mary Cairncross
rainforest reserve and the covered galleries and cafes there stay pleasant in the
wet. In town, the Hastings Street restaurants, day spas and the cinema fill a grey
day. A river cruise is still comfortable under cover when the beach is out. Summer
storms are usually short and clear quickly.

## Campgrounds and facilities

Holiday parks sit along the Noosa River and through the wider Sunshine Coast at
Coolum, Peregian and Tewantin, with powered sites, camp kitchens, dump points and
laundries; the riverfront parks are prized and book out far ahead for summer and
school holidays. Some beachside reserves have height and length limits, so check
your rig fits. Hastings Street parking is tight — arrive early or park back and
walk in.

## Local tips and FAQs

Noosa is busiest and priciest over the Queensland and southern school holidays and
across summer, when traffic into Hastings Street slows and parking vanishes by
mid-morning. The subtropical sun is strong most of the year, so carry water, shade
and reef-safe sunscreen. Watch for afternoon storms in summer. Book restaurants and
river tours ahead in peak weeks.
