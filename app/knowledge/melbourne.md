# Melbourne

## Laneways, coffee and culture

Melbourne's centre rewards walking: the laneways off Flinders and Little Collins
are lined with cafes, street art and small bars, and the city's coffee culture is
a genuine draw. The free City Circle tram loops the main sights, and Federation
Square, the State Library and the NGV anchor the arts precinct. Trams reach most
of the inner city on a Myki card, which beats driving a large rig into the grid.

## Parks, gardens and the Yarra

The Royal Botanic Gardens and the Tan track sit just south of the river, with the
Shrine of Remembrance above them. The Yarra's paths link the city to Southbank's
riverside dining and, further out, to Fairfield's boathouse. Carlton Gardens
frames the Royal Exhibition Building and the Melbourne Museum. These green spaces
give an easy, low-cost day between the city's indoor attractions.

## Markets and day trips

The Queen Victoria Market is the classic stop for produce, deli goods and, in
summer, a night market. The city is also a launchpad: the Yarra Valley wineries
and the Dandenong Ranges lie an hour east, Phillip Island's penguin parade to the
south-east, and the Great Ocean Road begins south-west. Mornington Peninsula's
beaches and hot springs make another comfortable day out.

## Wet-weather options

Melbourne's weather is famously changeable, so an indoor plan is always handy: the
NGV, Melbourne Museum, the Immigration Museum and Scienceworks are all under
cover. The arcades and the Emporium and QV centres link blocks of shops and
eateries without stepping outside. Sports fans can tour the MCG. Trams keep you
dry between stops when the sky opens, which it often does without warning.

## Campgrounds and facilities

There is no inner-city camping; holiday parks ring the suburbs — toward the
Mornington Peninsula south, Melton and the west, and the eastern suburbs — with
powered sites, dump points and laundries. Book ahead for school holidays and event
weekends, of which Melbourne has many. Inner-city clearways, tram lanes and the
hook-turn rules make driving a campervan through the centre stressful; base out
and take the train or tram in.

## Local tips and FAQs

Melbourne can serve four seasons in a day — carry a layer and a rain shell
whatever the forecast. Major events (the tennis, the football, the spring
carnival) spike prices and crowds, so check the calendar. Watch the CBD's tram
lanes, clearways and hook turns in a larger vehicle. Book popular restaurants
ahead, and use Myki-based public transport rather than hunting for city parking.
