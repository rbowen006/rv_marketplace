# Blue Mountains

## Lookouts and the Three Sisters

Echo Point at Katoomba frames the Three Sisters and the Jamison Valley, and is
the region's signature view — best early before the coach tours arrive. Nearby
Scenic World runs a railway, cableway and skyway over the valley for those who
would rather ride than walk. Govetts Leap at Blackheath and the Wentworth Falls
lookouts give big escarpment views with far fewer crowds a short drive on.

## Bushwalks and waterfalls

Trails range from ten-minute lookout strolls to full-day valley descents. The
Prince Henry Cliff Walk links Katoomba's lookouts along the rim; the Grand
Canyon loop at Blackheath drops into cool rainforest; and the Wentworth Falls
tracks pass a series of cascades. Carry water and a warm layer — the plateau is
cooler and wetter than the coast, and afternoon cloud can close in quickly.

## Villages and food

Leura's tree-lined street is full of cafes, providores and antique shops, and
Blackheath and Mount Victoria keep a quieter mountain-village feel. Katoomba has
the widest choice of eateries and the region's supplies. The cool climate suits
long lunches and open fires in winter; many kitchens close earlier than in the
city, so plan dinner rather than assume late service.

## Wet-weather and cool-season options

Mist and rain are part of the mountains and can make the lookouts atmospheric
rather than ruined. On genuinely wet days the Katoomba and Leura cafes, the
Everglades historic garden's tearoom, and indoor galleries fill the gap. Winter
brings frost and occasional snow up high; Yulefest sees venues run Christmas-in-
July menus. Pack warm and waterproof layers whatever the forecast.

## Campgrounds and facilities

Holiday parks cluster around Katoomba and Blackheath with powered sites, camp
kitchens and laundries, and there are national-park campgrounds at Euroka and in
the Megalong Valley for a wilder stay. Nights are cold at elevation year-round, so
check your rig's heating. Dump points are at the larger parks. Mountain roads are
narrow and winding in places — take the descents slowly in a heavy vehicle.

## Local tips and FAQs

The mountains are an easy day trip from Sydney, so weekends and school holidays
are busy at Echo Point and Scenic World — arrive early for parking and quieter
tracks. Weather changes fast and temperatures drop sharply after dark; always
carry water, a warm layer and a headtorch on walks. Fuel and groceries are easiest
in Katoomba. Check track status after heavy rain, as valley trails can close for
landslips.
