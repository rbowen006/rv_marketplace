# Newcastle

## Beaches and ocean baths

Newcastle strings a run of surf beaches along the city edge. Nobbys and Newcastle
Beach are patrolled and central; Bar Beach and Merewether catch the best surf and
sit below grassy headlands. The Merewether Ocean Baths and the smaller Newcastle
Baths give sheltered laps when the swell is up. The Bathers Way clifftop path
links them all, from Nobbys Lighthouse south to Glenrock, with lookouts the whole
way.

## City, harbour and history

The old coal port has turned its working waterfront into a walkable strip of
bars, galleries and restaurants. Fort Scratchley above the harbour entrance has
tunnels and gun emplacements to tour and one of the best harbour views in town.
Honeysuckle's boardwalk is the place for a waterside meal, and the city's
street-art laneways and the Newcastle Museum fill an easy afternoon on foot.

## Day trips: Hunter and Port Stephens

Newcastle sits between two big draws. The Hunter Valley vineyards are under an
hour west for cellar doors, long lunches and cheese. Port Stephens to the north
has the Stockton sand dunes, dolphin-watching cruises at Nelson Bay and calmer
bay beaches. Both make comfortable day trips, though a designated driver or a tour
is wise for the wineries.

## Wet-weather options

A wet day suits the Newcastle Museum, the Art Gallery and the harbourside cafes
and breweries. The Honeysuckle and Darby Street precincts keep you under cover
between stops. Fort Scratchley's tunnels are atmospheric in any weather. The ocean
baths are still swimmable in light rain if the surf is calm, and the drive out to
the Hunter cellar doors is an easy rainy-day plan.

## Campgrounds and facilities

Holiday parks sit around Stockton across the harbour, at Redhead and Blacksmiths
to the south, and toward Port Stephens north, with powered sites, dump points and
laundries. Stockton's park looks straight back at the city skyline. Book ahead for
summer and long weekends. Central Newcastle parking is metered and time-limited,
so basing at a park and driving in for the day works better with a larger rig.

## Local tips and FAQs

Newcastle is quieter and cheaper than Sydney but fills over summer and during
events at the beaches and along Honeysuckle. Surf beaches carry rips — swim
between the flags and use the ocean baths when the swell is big. Sun protection
matters spring to autumn. If you plan on Hunter wineries, build in a sober driver
or a tour, and check cellar-door hours, as many close mid-afternoon.
