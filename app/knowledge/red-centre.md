# Red Centre

## Alice Springs and the town

Alice Springs is the hub of the Centre: the supply town for fuel, groceries,
mechanics and water before any desert leg. The Telegraph Station, the Royal
Flying Doctor base and the Araluen arts precinct tell the town's history, and
Anzac Hill gives a sunset view over the ranges. Use Alice to stock up fully — the
distances between towns out here are long and services sparse.

## Gorges and the West MacDonnells

West of town the MacDonnell Ranges hide a chain of gorges and waterholes —
Simpsons Gap, Standley Chasm, Ellery Creek and Ormiston Gorge — many with short
walks and cool swimming holes that are a relief in the heat. The Larapinta Trail
threads the ranges for serious walkers. Waterholes can be very cold and some are
seasonal, so check conditions and carry more water than you think you need.

## Uluru, Kata Tjuta and Kings Canyon

The Centre's icons sit a few hours south-west: Uluru and Kata Tjuta in the
national park, and Kings Canyon on the way. Sunrise and sunset transform the rock,
and the base walk circles it on the flat. These are living cultural sites for the
Anangu — follow the signs on where not to photograph or climb. Distances are
large, so plan fuel stops and driving times carefully.

## Weather, seasons and wet-weather notes

The desert swings hard: hot days and cold nights, with summer daytime heat that
makes midday walking dangerous. The cooler months, roughly April to September, are
the comfortable window. Rain is rare but can flood dirt roads and fill the gorges
dramatically; storms pass quickly. On a hot or wet day, the Alice museums, the
Desert Park and the arts centres give an air-conditioned or covered alternative.

## Campgrounds and facilities

Holiday parks in Alice Springs and the resort area near Uluru have powered sites,
camp kitchens, dump points, laundries and pools; out along the ranges, national-
park and station campgrounds are basic — bring water, fuel and supplies. Book the
Uluru-area sites well ahead in the cool season. Some access roads are unsealed and
corrugated, so check whether your rig and tyres suit before leaving the bitumen.

## Local tips and FAQs

Carry plenty of drinking water everywhere and never rely on finding it; start
walks at dawn and avoid the midday heat, which can be lethal in summer. Fuel up at
every town, as gaps between servos are long. Respect signage at cultural sites and
around sacred areas. Nights are cold even after hot days, so pack warm layers.
Check road conditions after rain before taking any unsealed route.
