# Byron Bay

## Beaches and swimming

Main Beach sits right in town and is patrolled, with gentle water good for
families and first-time surfers. Wategos is a sheltered north-facing cove popular
for longboarding and calm morning swims. The Pass throws long, crowded surf
lines; Tallow Beach, south of the lighthouse, is wilder and quieter but not
always patrolled. Swim between the flags — rips are common on the open stretches.

## Walks and lookouts

The Cape Byron walking track loops from the town beaches up to the lighthouse,
the most easterly point of mainland Australia, and back — around two hours with
big ocean views. Early morning is the time for dolphins below the cliffs, and for
whales during the winter migration. The lighthouse precinct itself is an easy
drive-up if you'd rather skip the climb.

## Markets and food

The town has a strong cafe and wholefoods scene, busiest for weekend brunch. The
Byron Farmers Market runs Thursday mornings; larger craft and produce markets
rotate through nearby Bangalow and the surrounding towns on set weekends. Book
dinner ahead in peak season, when popular spots fill early.

## Wet-weather options

A rainy day suits the hinterland: the drive up to Nightcap National Park and the
Minyon Falls lookout is short and green, and the falls run hardest after rain.
The nearby villages of Bangalow and Federal have covered cafes and small
galleries. In town, the day spas and indoor studios book out when the weather
turns, so call ahead.

## Campgrounds and facilities

Holiday parks around Byron have powered sites, camp kitchens and laundries and
are among the most heavily booked on the coast — reserve far ahead for summer,
Easter and festival weekends. Some beachfront reserves have height and length
limits, so check your rig fits. Dump points are at the larger parks. Parking in
town is tight and metered; arriving early or staying out and walking in helps.

## Local tips and FAQs

Byron is busiest over summer, Easter and during festivals, when traffic into town
slows to a crawl mid-morning — start early. It can be noticeably pricier than
surrounding towns for both food and sites. The sun and surf are strong; take
water, shade and reef-safe sunscreen. Respect the flagged areas and local surf
etiquette at crowded breaks like The Pass.
