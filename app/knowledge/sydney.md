# Sydney

## Beaches and swimming

Bondi is the headline beach — patrolled, busy, and backed by cafes, with the
sheltered south end kinder for families and the north end better for surfers.
The Bondi-to-Coogee coastal walk strings together Tamarama, Bronte and Clovelly,
each with its own ocean pool. Manly, a ferry ride across the harbour, has a long
flat stretch good for beginners. Swim between the flags; the open ocean beaches
carry rips, while the harbour beaches like Shelly and Balmoral stay calm.

## Harbour and city

The harbour is the city's centre of gravity: the Opera House, the bridge, and a
public ferry network that doubles as the best-value sightseeing in town. The
Royal Botanic Garden and Mrs Macquarie's Point give the classic bridge-and-house
view for free. Darling Harbour and The Rocks pack museums, markets and bars into
easy walking distance, and a ferry to Watsons Bay pairs well with a cliff-top
walk at The Gap.

## Day trips and markets

The city is a launchpad: the Blue Mountains are about ninety minutes west, the
Royal National Park's coast track sits to the south, and the Northern Beaches run
up to Palm Beach. Weekend markets rotate through Paddington, Rozelle and the
Carriageworks farmers market in Redfern. Public transport reaches most of this on
an Opal card, which is often easier than driving a large rig into the inner city.

## Wet-weather options

Rain is a museum day: the Australian Museum, the Art Gallery of NSW and the
Maritime Museum at Darling Harbour are all indoors and central. The Queen
Victoria Building and the arcades off George Street are dry places to browse and
eat. Sea Life Sydney Aquarium and the nearby attractions suit families when the
weather turns. Ferries still run in the rain and stay a scenic, sheltered outing.

## Campgrounds and facilities

Sydney has no camping in the inner city; holiday parks sit on the outskirts — to
the north around the Northern Beaches, west toward Penrith, and south near the
Royal National Park — with powered sites, dump points and laundries. Book ahead
for school holidays and long weekends. Many inner-city streets have height limits
and tight metered parking, so basing yourself at a park and taking the train or
ferry in is the easier play with a campervan.

## Local tips and FAQs

Traffic and tolls make driving across the city slow at peak hours; the Opal
network avoids most of it. Beach parking fills early on hot weekends. The sun is
strong from spring to autumn — carry water, shade and reef-safe sunscreen. Book
popular restaurants and any Opera House performance ahead in peak season, and
check clearance signs before taking a tall rig into car parks or under the harbour
tunnels.
