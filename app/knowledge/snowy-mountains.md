# Snowy Mountains

## Winter: snow and the resorts

Thredbo and nearby Perisher are the main snowfields, running lifts through the
winter season for skiing, boarding and beginner lessons. Thredbo village sits at
the base with hire shops, cafes and the chairlift up to the alpine walks. Buy lift
passes ahead in peak weeks, carry chains and check road and snow reports before
driving up — conditions on the Alpine Way change fast in winter.

## Summer: alpine walks and Kosciuszko

Out of snow season the same country opens up for walking. From the top of the
Thredbo chairlift, the track to the summit of Mount Kosciuszko — the highest
point on the mainland — is a manageable day walk across the tops past glacial
lakes. Lower trails follow the Thredbo River and the wildflower meadows that peak
in January and February. Weather turns quickly at altitude even in summer, so
carry layers.

## Lakes, rivers and towns

Below the peaks, Lake Jindabyne and Lake Eucumbene draw anglers for trout and
paddlers in the warmer months, and Jindabyne is the region's supply town for fuel,
groceries and gear. The historic village of Charlotte Pass and the mountain-town
feel of Cooma and Adaminaby round out the area. The Snowy scheme's dams and the
high plains make for long, scenic drives.

## Wet-weather and off-season options

Cloud and rain roll across the mountains in any season. On low days, Jindabyne's
cafes, the Snowy Region visitor centre and the fishing and gear shops fill the
gap, and a lakeside drive stays worthwhile. In the shoulder seasons some village
services run reduced hours, so check what is open before relying on it. Warm,
waterproof layers are essential year-round.

## Campgrounds and facilities

Holiday parks and cabins cluster around Jindabyne and Cooma, with powered sites,
camp kitchens and laundries; national-park campgrounds sit along the rivers and at
the high plains for a wilder stay. Nights are cold even in summer and bitter in
winter, so heating and warm bedding matter. Dump points are at the larger parks.
In winter, park low and check whether your rig and route can handle snow and ice.

## Local tips and FAQs

Peak winter weekends are busy and priced accordingly; book accommodation, lift
passes and hire well ahead. National-park entry fees apply, higher in the snow
season. Carry chains in winter and know how to fit them. Altitude and fast weather
changes mean walkers should always take water, warm layers and sun protection —
the alpine sun is fierce even when the air is cold.
